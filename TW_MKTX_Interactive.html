Strategic positioning

Tradeweb plays

  • Multi-asset rates dominance. No competitor approaches TW in UST, EGB, swaps, mortgages, repo. $1.09B rates revenue (53% of total).
  • LSEG integration. LCH SwapClear pipe, FTSE Russell benchmark distribution (TW source for World Government Bond Index from 2025), Workspace data distribution, Microsoft 10-yr partnership.
  • Brand unification 2025. Dealerweb / TW Direct / ICD being retired as separate brands → one Tradeweb brand across 4 client sectors.
  • Frontier markets. Saudi ATS for Sukuk, Brazilian/Malaysian swaps, Mexican repo, BondConnect/CIBM Direct/Swap Connect (Asia), Yieldbroker (Australia).
  • Digital assets first-mover. Canton Network ($10M+ FY25 revenue), tokenized UST repo against USDC live transaction, Fnality, GS DAP, Alphaledger.
  • Net spotting differentiator. No MKTX equivalent. $3.75B+ IG ADV in 1Q26.
  • Corporate treasury. ICD acquisition unique. $96M FY25 revenue (+121.8%). 2025: USTs added to ICD Portal.

MarketAxess plays

  • Open Trading® network. 1,800 firms, $1.2T FY25 volume, 37% of eligible credit, $496M price improvement. Deepest A2A network in credit.
  • Credit specialism. Founded 2000 as IG specialist; remains credit-first. Larger D2C credit network than TW.
  • EM and international leadership. 32.8% of revenue international (vs 29.1% in 2021). 90 countries, 6,000 international traders. EM+eurobonds 40.5% of credit ADV. 30 local currencies via 200 dealers. 2025 first electronic Indian government bonds.
  • X-Pro UI investment. Next-gen trader interface. 1Q25: 85% of PT volume on X-Pro. Pilot for RFQ in Europe ongoing.
  • CP+ data distribution. S&P Global Bond Reference Data integration (2024). FactSet Workstation (2025).
  • Block + AI dealer selection. 2024: AI dealer selection for blocks. ADX (175 firms) extends automation to block sizes. 1Q26 block $7B ADV (+35%).
  • RFQ-hub cross-asset. Q2 2025 majority stake. Bilateral multi-asset RFQ for ETF and derivatives. Pragma (2023) for equities + FX algos.

Where they overlap most — and where they diverge

Convergence (overlap)

  • US IG credit: direct head-to-head. TW 25% share, MKTX 16.9% share. Both growing PT. Both running A2A. Both deploying ML pricing and automation.
  • Session-based protocols: TW Sweep / Mid-X (EU dominant) vs MKTX Mid-X matching (2025 US re-launch +163%). Same architecture, parallel deployments.
  • Composite pricing: TW Composite+ vs MKTX CP+. Both real-time, multi-asset. CP+ has stronger external distribution (S&P, FactSet); Composite+ has stronger internal-platform integration.
  • Auto-execution: TW AiEX vs MKTX Auto-X / ADX. Different brand sophistication levels but both targeting 60%+ no-touch on small/mid IG flow.
  • Munis (institutional): TW Institutional + TW Direct vs MKTX MuniBrokers + ICE Bonds connectivity. Multi-platform reality persists.

Divergence (uncontested ground)

  • TW only: rates ($1.09B revenue), swaps ($500M+ milestone), money markets via ICD ($174M), digital assets (Canton, $10M+), iNAV for ETFs, Net Spotting, r8fin futures-vs-cash, multi-asset PT bridging credit + UST + ETFs.
  • MKTX only: the deepest credit A2A network (Open Trading® at 1,800 firms vs Blast/AllTrade), the strongest EM dollar credit + EM local currency franchise, X-Pro UI investment focus, Pragma (equities + FX algos) cross-asset extension, RFQ-hub bilateral ETF + derivs RFQ.
  • TW dominant: rates breadth, multi-asset cross-sell, LSEG integration, frontier markets, digital assets first-mover.
  • MKTX dominant: credit A2A network density, EM + international franchise, credit-specific UX (X-Pro).
